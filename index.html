Piece Background Information

Title: Ride of the Valkyries

From: Die Walküre (The Valkyrie) - Part of The Ring Cycle

Composer: Richard Wagner (1813-1883)

Musical Period: Romantic

Instrumentation: Opera orchestra (large symphony orchestra with brass, strings, and woodwinds)

Where is it from: Composed in Germany. First performed in Munich, 1870.

Language: German (no lyrics in this instrumental section)

Fun Fact: This music is famously used in movies when something dramatic or powerful happens — like in Apocalypse Now (helicopter attack scene)!

More important info

Ride of the Valkyries is one of the most famous pieces from the Romantic period.

I can tell it is a Romantic piece because:

  • It has very strong emotions and dramatic contrasts (loud and soft parts).
  • The orchestra is huge and powerful, especially the brass section.
  • It tells a story from mythology (Valkyries are warrior maidens who carry fallen heroes to Valhalla).
  • There are big dynamic changes — it starts intense and keeps building.

This piece is significant because it is one of the most recognizable and powerful pieces in classical music. It has been used in countless movies, video games, and commercials to create an epic atmosphere.
